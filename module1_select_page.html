Module Selection

Learning Objectives for Module 1-A: Commodity Futures Overview

  • Overview of commodity short and long positions in a commodity futures market
  • The difference between a commodity futures contract and a forward contract
      • Centralized exchanges
      Standardized contracts
      Clearing house
      Contract offsetting
  • Role of margin and margin accounts
  • Marking to market accounting

Learning Objectives for Module 1-B: Pricing Relationships

  • Price discovery through profit seeking by speculators
  • Supply, demand and other fundanmental determinants of commodity prices
  • Stocks to use ratio
  • Costs and benefits of storage
  • The supply of storage and intertemporal pricing
  • Inter-year pricing with and without stock carry over
  • Introduction to the simulation model, including calibration
  • Simulation results

Learning Objectives for Module 1-C: Relationship between Spot and Futures Prices

  • Basis defined and broken down into time and location components
  • Convergence of spot and futures price due to arbitrage
  • Seasonal patterns in the basis: empirical evidence
  • The forward curve with normal price expectations
  • Estimation of price discounts between local and export markets
  • Seasonality in the basis due to seasonal production and storage
  • Basis patterns with and without stock carry over

Learning Objectives for Module 1-D: Hedging with Commodity Futures

  • Case study of price risk and hedging
  • Definition and applications of a short and long hedge
  • Connecting the profit from hedging with the change in the basis
  • Illustrating the concept of a strengthening and weakening basis
  • Associating profits from hedging with results from the simulation model
  • Relaxing the assumption of normal price expectations: backwardation and contango
